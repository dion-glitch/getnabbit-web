Overview

Nabbit is designed with privacy as its core principle. Items you save stay on your device. We do not create user accounts, we do not track you, and we do not sell or share your data.

What data is stored on your device

All items you save in Nabbit (addresses, phone numbers, WiFi passwords, dates, amounts, and custom categories) are stored locally on your iPhone using SQLite. This data never leaves your device except as described below.

  • Saved items (text, type, source app, sender, tags)
  • Custom categories (name, color)
  • App preferences (onboarding status, filters)

When data leaves your device

Nabbit's core functionality is fully on-device. There are three specific features that may send data off-device, all of which route through our own Cloudflare Worker proxy so no third-party API key lives in the App:

  • Automatic title summarization (Share Extension fallback): When you share text to Nabbit and our on-device regex cannot extract a clean title, the sanitized text is sent through our proxy to the Claude API (Anthropic) to generate a short title. This only happens when on-device extraction has nothing usable to offer.
  • Today tab: If you open the Today tab, sanitized metadata from items saved in the last 7 days may be sent through our proxy to Claude to identify what's time-relevant today.
  • Nabbit kort (shared cards): When you explicitly choose to share an item as a Nabbit kort link, the sanitized item text and type are stored on our Cloudflare D1 database for 30 days and are accessible via a unique link. This is the only feature that stores data outside your device, and it only happens when you actively tap "Share as Nabbit kort".

Before any text leaves the device, Nabbit's sanitizer removes sensitive patterns: credit card numbers, Swedish personal identification numbers (personnummer), passwords, PIN codes, BankID references, and CVV/security codes. The same sanitizer runs again on our server as a second line of defense.

Legal basis for processing

Under the GDPR, we process your data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance: Storing items locally and providing search — the core service you install the App for.
  • Legitimate interest: Using Claude for title summarization when on-device regex fails, so items have readable titles in your library.
  • Consent: The Today tab and Nabbit kort features — used only when you actively open the tab or tap the share button.

International data transfers and processors

When you use features that rely on third-party services, data may be transferred outside the EU/EEA. The following processors are used:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. (USA): Operates our Claude API proxy (Workers) and hosts shared Nabbit kort (D1 database). Processes sanitized item text and metadata. Cloudflare Workers run on a global edge network.
  • Anthropic PBC (USA): Processes sanitized text sent through our proxy for title summarization (Share Extension fallback) and the Today tab. Anthropic does not use API traffic for model training.
  • Web3Forms (Statichunt, USA): Processes your name, email, and message when you contact support via the web form.

These transfers are protected by the service providers' data processing agreements and EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs). Only sanitized data is sent — never raw text containing patterns that our sanitizer is configured to filter.

Siri integration

Nabbit uses Apple's App Intents framework to let you search your saved items by voice ("Hey Siri, search Nabbit for WiFi"). Siri processes your voice command on-device. Nabbit only receives the final search query text, not your audio. Nothing is sent to our servers for Siri searches.

Share Extension

When you share text to Nabbit from another app, the text is first processed locally on your device using regex patterns to extract addresses, phone numbers, WiFi credentials, dates, amounts, and other structured data. If — and only if — the on-device extractor cannot produce a usable title, the sanitized text is sent via our Cloudflare Worker proxy to the Claude API to generate a short title (see "When data leaves your device" above). The original raw text is always stored locally on your device regardless.

Data we do NOT collect

  • We do not collect analytics or usage data
  • We do not use tracking or advertising frameworks
  • We do not create user accounts or profiles
  • We do not access your contacts, photos, or other apps
  • We do not sell or share your data with third parties

How to delete your data

You can delete individual items by swiping left on any item in the library. To delete all data at once, go to Settings > Data > Delete all data. This permanently removes all saved items and categories from your device.

Since no data is stored externally, deleting data from the app removes it completely.

Your rights under GDPR

If you are in the EU/EEA, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we process about you.
  • Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data (you can also do this directly in the App).
  • Restriction: Request that we limit how we process your data.
  • Data portability: Request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interest.

Since all your data is stored locally on your device, most of these rights are already in your hands. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@getnabbit.app.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Sweden, this is Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten (IMY) — imy.se.

Children's privacy

Nabbit is not directed at children under 13, in accordance with the Swedish implementation of GDPR Article 8. We do not knowingly collect information from children.
